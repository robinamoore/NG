Urban oasis

When we moved in to our new house the garden was a small tricky shaped mud patch – very daunting. Our brief to Dan was for a child friendly but visually interesting garden with height and interest to break up the hard lines of the surrounding fences and buildings.

Dan really took the whole family’s needs for the garden in to consideration and created a design that we all love. He cleverly zoned the garden which makes the small space feel more spacious and interesting and gives both adults and kids their own areas of the garden. He created a stunning central raised bed and path configuration which is a beautiful centre point to the garden. To one side of this is a cosy little lawn area surrounded by fruit trees and the children’s pretty play house. To the other side is a more spacious lawn flanked by silver birch on which the children have room to play. Probably the most notable aspect of the garden is the excellent planting scheme that Dan created for us. As novice gardeners we knew that we wanted a seaside/cottage garden feel, but we would have been totally lost with selecting the right plants. Dan expertly selected plants that give height, softness, movement and colour.

The garden is now over 3 years old and the planting has matured really well, with Dan’s guidance on maintenance being invaluable. Thank you!

Excellent planting scheme

I’m proud to say I was one of Dan’s first customers in Brighton!

My garden is 80 feet long with difficult grassy areas and backs on to woodland – and it would be fair to say that when Dan arrived on the scene it was a tad wild! About a third of it was clogged with brambles and the rest was out of hand. The “soil” is mostly chalk and the garden is frequently assaulted by foxes and badgers.

Dan’s brief was to clear the brambles; create a wild garden with alliums and daffodils adjacent to the woods; and maintain the rest of the garden – without disturbing Nature too much, as wild flowers, insects and other creatures are important to me. This he has done with great sensitivity for many years – never being too brutal, but being firm enough to keep things in order! I’m not the world’s greatest gardener so have appreciated his advice and input as well as his ability to put into practice what I have had in mind.

Dan is an extremely hard worker and can do in a morning what it would take me days to do. We have coined the phrase “the garden has been Danned” for after his visits, when everything is orderly and peaceful and the garden basks under his attention. Even the odd cup of tea from me doesn’t seem to have stopped him from getting things done!

In addition to his gardening skills and knowledge, Dan is reliable, honest, trustworthy and kind – all things of great value when you are choosing someone to do such an important job for you. I for one will miss him dreadfully. Lucky Norfolk!
